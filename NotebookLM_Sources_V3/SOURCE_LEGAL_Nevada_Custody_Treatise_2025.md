# Nevada Child Custody Jurisprudence: An Analytical Treatise
## NRS Chapters 125A and 125C, Modification Standards, and Evolving Statutory Protections

### Core Pillars
1. **Public Policy (NRS 125C.001):** Focus on stability and frequent association with both parents.
2. **Gender Neutrality:** Statutes prohibit preference based on gender.
3. **Parental Preference (NRS 125C.004):** Presumption favors natural parents over third parties.

### The Best Interests Matrix (NRS 125C.0035)
12 statutory factors, including:
- Wishes of the child (age/capacity evaluated).
- Cooperation and association (active gatekeeping is penalized).
- Mental/Physical health (disability/medical marijuana cannot be sole basis for denial).
- **Domestic Violence:** Clear/convincing finding triggers rebuttable presumption against custody.

### The Romano Unified Standard (Post-2022)
*Romano v. Romano* unified modification requirements. Whether Joint or Primary, the moving party MUST prove:
1. A **Substantial Change in Circumstances** since the last order.
2. Modification is in the **Best Interest** of the child.
*Strategic Note:* Prevents serial filings and protects the durability of the initial decree.

### Relocation (NRS 125C.0065 / 125C.007)
Requires:
- Written consent or court order.
- Sensible, good-faith reason.
- Best interest showing.

### SB 275 (2025) - THE PROTECTIVE PARENT SHIELD
This is the most critical update for the Architect:
- **No Removal for "Relationship Improvement":** Court cannot remove a child from a safe, bonded, protective parent just to "improve" a relationship with the other parent.
- **Reunification Treatment Restrictions:** Prohibits "isolation" from the protective parent. Treatment must be scientifically validated.
- **Expert Evidence:** Only professionals with *clinical victim-advocacy* experience can provide expert evidence on domestic violence/abuse—not just forensic evaluators.
- **Parental Alienation:** Curtails the misuse of alienation claims against protective parents.

### AB 344 (2025)
- Mandatory counsel for parents/children in protective custody.
- Modernizes "Master" to "Judicial Officer".

### Jurisdictional (UCCJEA - NRS 125A)
- Exclusive, continuing jurisdiction remains in Nevada unless specific connection/residency tests are failed.
